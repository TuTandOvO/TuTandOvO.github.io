Does DNA explain why I'm the heavier twin?

My fraternal twin brother and I each had a ~60× genome sequenced. I compared them honestly — zygosity, ~1.8M differing variants, obesity-gene panel, and a genome-wide BMI polygenic score. Here is what the data actually say.

The honest read

We are confirmed fraternal twins who share about half our DNA, and yet none of the genetic signals explain why I have been the heavier one: no obesity-gene variant differs, our genome-wide BMI polygenic scores are essentially identical (and nominally higher in my leaner brother), and the few classic BMI variants that differ do not favour me. The most likely driver of a lifelong difference is therefore non-genetic — early-life environment, behaviour and epigenetics — none of which whole-genome sequencing can see. A well-built null is the result.
